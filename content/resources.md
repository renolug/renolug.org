New to Linux, or just looking for good references? Here's a starter kit. This
list is intentionally short and opinionated — suggestions are welcome on the
[mailing list](https://groups.google.com/g/renolug).

## Getting started with Linux

## Pick a distribution

- [Rocky Linux](https://rockylinux.org/) — enterprise-grade, RHEL-compatible.
- [Fedora](https://fedoraproject.org/) — modern, polished, close to upstream.
- [Debian](https://www.debian.org/) — the rock-solid universal OS.
- [Ubuntu](https://ubuntu.com/) — approachable and widely supported.
- [Arch Linux](https://archlinux.org/) — best customization without going source-based.
- [Gentoo](https://www.gentoo.org/) - ultimate customization for advanced users.

## Documentation & help

- [Rocky Linux Docs](https://docs.rockylinux.org) - collaborative docs for Enterprise Linux.
- [Arch Wiki](https://wiki.archlinux.org/) — the most actively maintained and complete Linux docs anywhere.
- [man pages](https://man7.org/linux/man-pages/) — the manual, online.
- [tldr pages](https://tldr.sh/) - too long, didn't read.
- [Unix & Linux Stack Exchange](https://unix.stackexchange.com/) — Q&A.

## Local & regional

- [BridgeWire Makerspace](https://bridgewire.org/) — a Reno maker community and
  a longtime friend of local tech gatherings.

## Open source, broadly

- [Open Source Initiative](https://opensource.org/) — stewards of the Open
  Source Definition.
- [Free Software Foundation](https://www.fsf.org/) — the free software
  movement's home base.
